What is HPRC?

The High Power Rocketry Club (HPRC) is a Subcommittee of WPI's chapter of the American Institute of Aeronautics & Astronautics (AIAA) that competes in high power rocketry competitions.  This year we were involved in the NASA University Student Launch Initiative, the same competition we competed in the past two years.  We also take a large portion of our time in the club to educate members through club ran workshops on topics such as rocketry, robotics, manufacturing, computer aided design and many more. If you are a WPI student who is interested in joining the team, please send our executive board an email by clicking here.

This Year's Competition

NASA Student Launch is a national competition where both college and middle/high school teams compete in their respective groups to design and build a high-powered rocket and payload capable of completing both a flight and a mission on the ground. Teams are scored based on various reports presented to NASA, the rocket's proximity to the estimated altitude, and the completion of the payload mission. For Student Launch 2021, the mission of the payload is to land and self-level itself to within 5 degrees of vertical. Once orientated it will then need to take a 360 degree panorama of the surrounding area.
